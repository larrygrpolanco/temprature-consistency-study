[1c] With advancing age, zebrafish have reduced regenerative capacity, accumulation of DNA damage, and cardiovascular impairment (Gilbert et al., 2014; Sun et al., 2014).
[1b] The shared consequences of aging in humans and zebrafish, combined with the low cost of hosting zebrafish for extended periods, suggests that zebrafish could serve as a valuable model organism in the study of aging.

[1b] Untangling the web of interactions between age, genetics, and sex, is facilitated by use of model organisms for which many molecular genetic tools are available such as zebrafish.
